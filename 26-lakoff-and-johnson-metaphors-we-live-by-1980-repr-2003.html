Lakoff And Johnson Metaphors We Live By 1980 Repr 2003

1. Problem Statement

  1. Lakoff and Johnson - Metaphors We Live By (1980, repr. 2003)

Focused Problem Statement: Treating metaphorical language like "attacking an argument" merely as figurative speech obscures its cognitive function, as modeled by Lakoff & Johnson (L3 Framing). The ARGUMENT IS WAR system (L1 Conceptual Metaphor) simulates argumentation using war concepts (L0 Embodied Experience), shaping speakers' reasoning and interaction (L2) in ways hidden by assuming literal meaning is primary (L4 Competing Linguistic View).

2. Core Model

26. Lakoff and Johnson - Metaphors We Live By (1980, repr. 2003)

  • MODEL: Argues that metaphor is not merely a poetic device but a fundamental aspect of human thought and language, structuring how we perceive, think, and act. Our conceptual systems are largely metaphorical, and these metaphors shape our understanding of abstract concepts (e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR, TIME IS MONEY).
  • Index:
    • ◻ Metaphor → ◻ (Not just Poetic Device) → ◻ Fundamental Cognitive/Linguistic Mechanism
    • ◻ Metaphor → ◻ Structuring Perception/Thought/Action
    • ◻ Human Conceptual System → ◻ Largely Metaphorical
    • ◻ Metaphors → ◻ Shaping Understanding of Abstract Concepts

3. Four-Level Analysis

26. Lakoff and Johnson - Metaphors We Live By (1980, repr. 2003)

System: Understanding and using the phrase "He attacked every weak point in my argument."

Level 0: <Reality> = <The Abstract Process of Argumentation (presenting claims, reasons, evidence)> & <Physical Experience of Warfare/Conflict>

Level 1: <Machine Layer> = <The Conceptual Metaphor System: ARGUMENT IS WAR>
<ARGUMENT IS WAR Metaphor> [simulates] → <Argument Structure> using <Concepts from War Domain (attack, defend, win, lose)>
<ARGUMENT IS WAR Metaphor> [outputs] → <Linguistic Expressions ("attack weak points," "defend position")> & <Inferential Patterns (seeing argument as zero-sum)>
<ARGUMENT IS WAR Metaphor> [filters] → <Other Ways of Understanding Argument (e.g., as collaboration, exploration)>

Level 2: <Human Use Layer> = <Speaker/Listener using the phrase>
<Speaker/Listener> [interprets] → <Argument Situation> through <The Lens of the WAR Metaphor (often unconsciously)>
<Speaker/Listener> [steers] → <Interaction> based on <Metaphorical Entailments (adversarial stance)>

Level 3: <Reflexive Framing Layer> = <Lakoff & Johnson (Cognitive Linguists)>
<Lakoff & Johnson> [model] → <Metaphor> not as mere words, but as <Conceptual Structures Shaping Thought & Reality>
<Lakoff & Johnson> [challenge ontology] → <Of Literal Meaning as Primary>, seeing <Metaphor as Fundamental>
<Lakoff & Johnson> [elevate] → <Embodied Experience> as <Source Domain for Abstract Concepts>

Level 4: <Latent & Systemic Evaluation> = <Embodied Cognition Paradigm / Systematicity of Conceptual Metaphor>
<Embodied Cognition Paradigm> [naturalizes] → <Link between Physical Experience & Abstract Thought>
<Systematicity of Metaphor> [obscures] → <Potential for Novel Metaphors or Non-Metaphorical Thought> (though acknowledged)
<Framework Logic> [reframes] → <Everyday Language> as <Window into Underlying Conceptual System>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

26. Lakoff and Johnson - Metaphors We Live By (1980, repr. 2003)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Human language, thought, and experience across cultures.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Conceptual systems and the role of metaphor in structuring understanding and reasoning.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Everyday language expressions, abstract concepts (time, love, argument, ideas), cognitive processes × Cultural contexts.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Metaphorical linguistic expressions (e.g., "attack a weak point," "waste time," "deep ideas"), patterns of inference based on metaphors, categorization behaviors. (ℝᵏ representing frequency of metaphorical expressions, consistency of entailments).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Novel metaphors, changes in cultural understanding, conscious reflection on metaphorical structures, different language systems.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Cognitive Linguistics, Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT), analysis of everyday language, rejection of traditional views of metaphor as purely linguistic/ornamental.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the pervasive, systematic, and often unconscious role of metaphor in basic thought processes.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily examines conceptual metaphors underlying ordinary language, arguing they structure thought itself.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Collection and analysis of common metaphorical expressions grouped by underlying conceptual metaphors (e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR, IDEAS ARE FOOD, TIME IS MONEY).
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Conceptual Metaphor – Metaphor is not just a matter of words but of thought; we understand and reason about abstract concepts primarily through metaphorical mappings from more concrete, embodied source domains (e.g., understanding Argument in terms of War). These conceptual metaphors are systematic and structure our reality.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Conceptual Metaphor Analysis.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Identifying underlying conceptual metaphors (TARGET DOMAIN IS SOURCE DOMAIN) by analyzing patterns of linguistic expressions and their inferential entailments.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Collections of everyday metaphorical linguistic expressions.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The underlying conceptual metaphor (e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR); the systematic mapping between source and target domains; the principle that metaphor structures thought.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting from individual words to the underlying conceptual mapping.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A theory of human conceptual systems as fundamentally metaphorical, grounded in embodied experience.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Identifying and analyzing numerous conceptual metaphors across various abstract domains.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Theories of embodied cognition, cognitive linguistics, categorization.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Systematicity of linguistic evidence, coherence of mappings, explanatory power for reasoning patterns (qualitative/structural).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Revealing the largely metaphorical nature of the human conceptual system.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Conceptual Metaphor Theory. Governs the analysis of language and thought. Binds Concrete Source Domains (Embodied Experience) DOMAIN ↔ Abstract Target Domains (Concepts) CODMAIN via metaphorical mappings.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (Conceptual Metaphors) | ◻ REALITY (Abstract concepts/Experience) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The metaphors are not just models of reality, they actively structure our experience of reality; the linguistic expressions are artifacts revealing the underlying conceptual model).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Concepts involving time, processes) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (Spatial metaphors are foundational). Many metaphors map time-like concepts onto space-like source domains (e.g., TIME IS A PATH).
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Experience, conceptual domains) | ◻ DISCRETE (Linguistic expressions, source/target mappings) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Analyzes discrete expressions to reveal the structure of continuous conceptual domains).
  • Operations: Observe everyday language use. Encode linguistic expressions as evidence for underlying conceptual metaphors. Entangle abstract reasoning (FEATURE) with concrete source domains (FEATURE) via metaphorical mappings. Critiques theories that Forget the cognitive role of metaphor.
