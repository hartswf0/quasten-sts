Vannevar Bush As We May Think 1945 Repr 2003

1. Problem Statement

  1. Vannevar Bush - "As We May Think" (1945, repr. 2003)

Focused Problem Statement: Viewing Bush's Memex (L1 Conceptual Machine) merely as a microfilm reader obscures its core innovation modeled in his vision (L3 Framing). The Memex aimed to simulate human associative thought (L0 Reality) via linking 'trails,' augmenting intellect (L4 Belief) for researchers (L2) – a concept obscured if focusing only on storage rather than the associative linking intended to overcome information overload.

2. Core Model

37. Vannevar Bush - "As We May Think" (1945, repr. 2003)

  • MODEL: Proposes a hypothetical personal device called the "Memex" that would augment human memory and intellect by storing vast amounts of information (books, records, communications) and allowing users to create associative trails (links) between related items, mimicking the associative nature of human thought. This envisioned precursor to hypertext and personal computing.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Information Overload → ◻ Problem for Human Intellect
    • ◻ Human Thought → ◻ Associative Nature
    • ◻ Proposed Solution → ◻ Memex (Hypothetical Device)
    • ◻ Memex → ◻ Stores Vast Information
    • ◻ Memex → ◻ Creates Associative Trails (Links) → ◻ Augmenting Human Memory/Intellect
    • ◻ Memex → ◻ Precursor to Hypertext/Personal Computing

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

37. Vannevar Bush - "As We May Think" (1945, repr. 2003)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Post-WWII scientific/technological landscape facing an information explosion.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The storage, retrieval, and utilization of the rapidly growing body of human knowledge (scientific record).
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Scientific literature, libraries, indexing systems, human memory, associative thought processes × Context of post-war technological optimism and information management challenges.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Vast amounts of documented knowledge (books, articles, microfilm), limitations of existing indexing/retrieval methods, speed and associative nature of human thought (as ideal). (ℝᵏ representing volume of information, retrieval time, relevance of results).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Development of new storage media (microfilm), envisioned future technologies (dry photography, vocoders, calculating machines).
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Perspective of a high-level science administrator, engineer's focus on practical solutions, belief in technological progress, analysis of information processing limitations.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the macro-problem of managing the totality of scientific knowledge and augmenting human intellect to cope with it.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Concerned with improving access and use of the existing record, envisions tools mimicking human associative thought, primarily for scientific/intellectual work.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Thought experiments involving microfilm, projection systems, keyboards, levers; critique of current library indexing; the conceptual design of the "Memex."
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: The Memex Vision – A hypothetical personal device ("memory extender") that would store vast amounts of information (books, records, correspondence) on microfilm, allowing the user to retrieve items quickly and, crucially, to create associative "trails" (links) between related items, thereby mimicking and augmenting the associative nature of human thought to combat information overload.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Problem/Solution Engineering Design / Visionary Analogy (Technology mimicking Mind).
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Identifying the limitations of existing information systems by comparing them to the speed and associativity of human thought, then proposing a technological solution based on analogy.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: The problem of information overload and inadequate retrieval methods.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The concept of associative indexing (linking); the Memex device as an integrated system; the goal of augmenting human intellect.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the structural problem of access and association, envisioning technological capabilities beyond the immediate state-of-the-art.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A detailed conceptual design and scenario for the Memex, illustrating its potential use.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Integrating various envisioned technologies (microfilm, dry photography, input/output devices) into the Memex system.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Analogy between machine linking and human associative thought, belief in solvable engineering problems, information theory precursors.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Speed of access, ease of creating associative trails, capacity of storage, ability to augment thought (design goals).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: The creation of practical technology (like the Memex) to effectively manage information and enhance human thinking.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Vision for Associative Information Systems / Human Augmentation. Governs the conceptual design of the Memex. Binds Human Associative Thought Processes DOMAIN ↔ Machine Information Storage/Linking Mechanisms CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (The Memex concept) | ◻ REALITY (Human mind OR later technologies like hypertext) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The Memex model is a powerful artifact that directly influenced later reality (hypertext development), making reality partly an artifact of Bush's model).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Following associative trails through information) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The storage medium, the conceptual space of linked documents). The Memex enables navigating information space in a time-like, associative way.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Flow of human thought) | ◻ DISCRETE (Microfilm frames, links, indexed items) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Proposes a discrete technological system to better support the brain's continuous, associative processes).
  • Operations: Observe the problem of information overload and the nature of human thought. Encode a technological solution (Memex) based on associative linking. Envisions Entangling disparate pieces of information (FEATURE) through user-created trails (FEATURE). Aims to help scientists Forget the limitations of linear indexing.
