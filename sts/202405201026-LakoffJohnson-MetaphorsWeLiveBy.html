Focused Problem Statement:

Traditional theories of language and meaning (Level 3 Framing) treat metaphor as a peripheral, purely linguistic or decorative device, assuming thought is primarily literal and propositional (Level 1 Model: Literal Meaning Primacy). This obscures the fundamental cognitive role of conceptual metaphor, where abstract concepts (Level 0 Reality, e.g., argument, time, love) are systematically understood and reasoned about via mappings from more concrete, embodied source domains (e.g., war, money, journeys - Level 4 Logic: Embodied Cognition). This oversight prevents recognition of how pervasive, conventional metaphors structure our everyday understanding, inferences, and actions (Level 2 Use/Interpretation).

1. Core Model and Index

Lakoff, G., & Johnson, M. (1980). Metaphors we live by. University of Chicago Press.

MODEL: Argues that metaphor is not merely a poetic device but a fundamental aspect of human thought and language, structuring our conceptual system and shaping how we understand and experience reality; everyday language reveals these underlying conceptual metaphors.

Index:

2. Four-Level Analysis

Illustrative Olog Analysis (Conceptual Metaphor TIME IS MONEY):

  • Level 0: Base World

* <Reality> = <Subjective experience of time passage, Need to coordinate activities, Economic exchange>

  • Level 1: Machine Layer

* <Conceptual Metaphor System (TIME IS MONEY mapping)> [simulates] → <Model structuring TIME domain using concepts from MONEY domain (resource, spending, wasting, saving)> (Problem: Traditional view sees this only as linguistic flourish, not cognitive model).

* <Metaphor System> [outputs] → <Systematic linguistic expressions ("waste time," "spend time," "budget time") & inferences (time is limited/valuable)>

* <Metaphorical Mapping> [filters] → <How time is conceptualized (emphasizing quantification/utility, hiding other aspects)> (Problem: How metaphor shapes understanding is missed).

  • Level 2: Human Use Layer

* <Language Users> [steer/interpret] → <Using/Understanding time-related language through the MONEY frame> (Problem: Unconscious cognitive structuring obscured).

* <Users> [act] → <Based on inferences from the metaphor (e.g., scheduling tightly 'not to waste time')>

  • Level 3: Reflexive Framing Layer

* <Lakoff & Johnson (Theorists)> [model] → <Thought/Language via Conceptual Metaphor> / [question] → <Literal meaning primacy, Objectivist semantics>

* <Traditional Semantics (Framing)> [models] → <Metaphor as deviation from literal meaning>

  • Level 4: Latent & Systemic Evaluation

* <Literal Meaning Primacy (Logic)> [obscures] → <Fundamental cognitive role of conventional metaphor> (Problem: Core mechanism of abstract thought misrepresented).

* <Mind-Body Dualism (Disposition)> [naturalizes] → <Separation of abstract thought from embodied experience (source domains)>

* <Conceptual Metaphor Theory> [reframes] → <Metaphor as central to cognition, grounded in embodiment> / [elevates] → <Systematic linguistic patterns as evidence of cognitive structure>

3. Spatiotemporal Framework

SPATIOTEMPORAL FRAMEWORK

◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Human language and cognition in everyday life.

→ [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The human conceptual system and its manifestation in language.

→ [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Cognitive processes × Linguistic expressions × Cultural contexts × Embodied experience.

→ [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Everyday language use, especially metaphorical expressions (e.g., "argument is war," "time is money," "love is a journey"), patterns of inference, ways of understanding abstract concepts. (ℝᵏ representing conceptual mappings, linguistic data frequencies).

→ [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Introduction of new metaphors, cultural shifts changing metaphor salience, poetic innovation extending metaphorical mappings, conscious reflection on metaphors.

→ [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Cognitive Linguistics, Conceptual Metaphor Theory.

→ [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the structure of everyday conceptual systems as revealed through conventional language use.

→ [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily focuses on conventional, systematic metaphors structuring thought, less on purely literary/poetic or highly idiosyncratic metaphors. Emphasizes cognitive function over purely aesthetic.

→ [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Analysis of common English expressions grouped by underlying conceptual metaphors (ARGUMENT IS WAR, THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS, IDEAS ARE FOOD, etc.).

→ [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Metaphor as not merely a linguistic device but a fundamental aspect of human thought, structuring our conceptual system, understanding, and experience, primarily by mapping concepts from concrete, embodied source domains onto more abstract target domains.

◻ ENGINE: Conceptual Metaphor Analysis.

→ [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Identifying systematic correlations in language use to reveal underlying conceptual metaphors (e.g., mapping aspects of WAR onto ARGUMENT).

→ [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: A specific metaphorical expression used in everyday language (e.g., "winning an argument," "attacking weak points," "defending a claim").

→ [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The underlying conceptual metaphor (e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR) that structures a whole system of related expressions and inferences.

→ [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting from literal meaning to identify the metaphorical mapping between source and target domains.

→ [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: The theory of conceptual metaphor; models of specific metaphors showing source-to-target mappings and entailments.

→ [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: How using expressions based on a metaphor reinforces the underlying conceptual mapping, making it feel natural and shaping future thought and action related to the target domain.

→ [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Cognitive science (conceptual structure), linguistics (semantics, pragmatics), philosophy (critique of objectivism, emphasis on embodiment).

→ [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Coherence and systematicity of the metaphorical mapping, explanatory power for linguistic patterns and inferences (qualitative linguistic analysis).

→ [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A comprehensive mapping of the major conceptual metaphors structuring a given language/culture's understanding of reality.

METAMODEL

Distinctions & Crossings:

◻ MODEL (The conceptual metaphor, e.g., ARGUMENT IS WAR) | ◻ REALITY (The actual nature of argument, which may or may not be adversarial) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The way we talk about, think about, and conduct arguments, shaped by the dominant metaphor; potential limitations or biases introduced by the metaphor).

◻ TIME-LIKE (Development of metaphorical systems, learning metaphors) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The structure of a conceptual metaphor mapping source to target domain features) → ◻ CAUSAL CONE (How the established metaphorical structures (space-like) shape understanding and inference processes over time).

◻ CONTINUOUS (Richness of experience in source/target domains) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific mappings highlighted by the metaphor; entailments derived) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (The metaphor selects and maps specific aspects of the source domain onto the target, highlighting some features and hiding others).

Operations: [Observe] everyday language use, looking for systematic patterns. [Encode] linguistic expressions as evidence for underlying conceptual metaphors. [Convolve] multiple expressions (PATCH) to reveal the common metaphorical structure (KERNEL). [Entangle] language, thought, culture, and embodied experience. [Forget] the traditional view of metaphor as purely decorative or literal language as primary/unproblematic.
