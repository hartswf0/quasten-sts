Focused Problem Statement:

Late liberal governance, particularly in settler colonial contexts, operates through a fundamental ontological distinction between Life (Bios) and Nonlife (Geos), termed "Geontology" (Level 1 Governing Model). This framework, presented as rational and universal (Level 3 Framing), systematically obscures its own political function ("Geontopower" - Level 4 Logic) in devaluing and dispossessing Indigenous peoples whose ontologies recognize the agency or significance of entities categorized as 'Nonlife' by liberalism (Level 0 Indigenous Reality). Legal and resource management decisions (Level 2 Use) based on this geontology thus enact ontological violence while masking it as neutral administration or the application of self-evident categories.
