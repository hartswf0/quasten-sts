Focused Problem Statement:

Traditional cognitive science (Level 3 Framing) predominantly models cognition as an individual, internal process residing solely within the human mind (Level 1 Model: "Mind = Brain"). This obscures the fundamental ways complex cognitive tasks, such as ship navigation (Level 0 Reality), are actually accomplished through distributed cognitive systems involving interaction between multiple people, external artifacts (charts, tools), environmental structures, and culturally transmitted procedures (Level 2 Use/Practice). By focusing only on the individual, this framework fails to recognize the system-level computation and the crucial cognitive functions performed by non-mental elements (Level 4 Logic: Methodological Individualism).

1. Core Model and Index

Hutchins, E. (1996). Cognition in the wild. MIT Press.

MODEL: Argues that cognition is not solely an individual, internal process but is fundamentally "distributed" across individuals, artifacts, and the environment within socio-cultural systems. Uses detailed ethnographic analysis of ship navigation to show how cognitive tasks are accomplished collectively through interaction within a complex system.

Index:

◻ Cognition → (is) → ◻ Distributed (across people, artifacts, environment)

◻ Cognitive System → (includes) → ◻ Internal / External Resources

◻ Culture → (shapes/organizes) → ◻ Cognitive Processes

◻ Ethnography (of practice, e.g., navigation) → (reveals) → ◻ Distributed Cognition

3. Spatiotemporal Framework

23. Hutchins, Edwin - Cognition in the Wild (1996)

SPATIOTEMPORAL FRAMEWORK

◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Socio-technical systems where goal-directed cognitive work occurs (specifically, naval ship navigation).

→ [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The processes of perception, calculation, representation, communication, and action involved in achieving a complex cognitive goal (navigating a ship).

→ [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Crew members × Physical environment × Artifacts (charts, tools, instruments) × Cultural practices/procedures × Time (unfolding of the navigation task).

→ [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Spoken commands, instrument readings, marks on charts, bodily movements, manipulations of tools, division of labor, established procedures. (ℝᵏ representing representational states across different media, information flow paths, task timings).

→ [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Equipment failure, unexpected environmental conditions (fog, currents), errors in communication or calculation, changes in procedure or personnel.

→ [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Cognitive Ethnography; Distributed Cognition framework.

→ [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Micro-analysis of information flow and representational transformations within the entire navigation team and its environment as a single computational system.

→ [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: The cognitive system is bounded by the contributing elements (people, tools, environment) involved in the task, not by the skin/skull of individuals.

→ [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Detailed transcription and analysis of communication, tool use, and movement during navigation tasks observed aboard US Navy ships.

→ [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Cognition as fundamentally "Distributed" – cognitive processes (memory, calculation, reasoning) are properties of the larger socio-technical system, emerging from the interactions between internal minds, external artifacts, environmental structures, and cultural practices, rather than residing solely within individual heads.

◻ ENGINE: Distributed Cognition Analysis / Cognitive Ethnography.

→ [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Analyzing the propagation and transformation of representational states across different media (minds, instruments, charts, verbal communication) within the cognitive system. Shifting the unit of analysis from individual to system.

→ [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: A specific moment in the navigation task involving communication, tool use, or calculation (e.g., taking a bearing, plotting a fix, discussing course).

→ [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: Identification of the functional cognitive system composed of multiple actors and artifacts; mapping the flow and transformation of information essential to task completion.

→ [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting the cognitive functions from their specific physical or individual instantiation to see how they are accomplished by the system as a whole.

→ [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A model of the navigation task as computation performed by a distributed cognitive system; detailed maps of information flow and representational state changes.

→ [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: How the reliable functioning of the distributed system (through procedures, tool design, training) allows complex cognitive tasks to be achieved routinely, reinforcing the system's structure. Learning is enculturation into the system.

→ [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Cognitive science (information processing, computation), anthropology (cultural practices, apprenticeship), ecological psychology (affordances), CSCW (computer-supported cooperative work).

→ [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Efficiency, reliability, robustness of the distributed cognitive system; mapping of computational pathways; identification of bottlenecks or failure points (functional system analysis).

→ [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A detailed functional explanation of how the complex cognitive task (navigation) is successfully accomplished by the integrated socio-technical system.

METAMODEL

Distinctions & Crossings:

◻ MODEL (The analyst's model of the distributed cognitive system and information flows) | ◻ REALITY (The actual, fluid, potentially messy performance of the navigation task by the crew) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (Breakdowns, errors, or workarounds observed during the task that highlight the functioning or limits of the system).

◻ TIME-LIKE (The sequential unfolding of the navigation process, step-by-step) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The physical layout of the bridge, the arrangement of tools, the social organization of the crew) → ◻ CAUSAL CONE (How the spatial arrangement of resources and social roles (space-like) shapes the flow of information and the sequence of actions over time).

◻ CONTINUOUS (Flow of real-world events, subtle adjustments) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific measurements taken, commands given, calculations made, points plotted on chart) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (The cognitive system's own methods (procedures, tools) for segmenting continuous reality into discrete representations needed for computation and decision-making).

Operations: [Observe] complex cognitive work in its natural setting via ethnography. [Encode] observations in terms of information processing, representational states, and media transformations across the system. [Convolve] individual actions/utterances (PATCH) with the overall system state/procedures (KERNEL) to understand their computational role. [Entangle] internal cognition, external artifacts, social organization, and cultural practices. [Forget] assuming cognition stops at the skull; methodological individualism.
