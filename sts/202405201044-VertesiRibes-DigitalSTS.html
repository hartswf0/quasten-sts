Focused Problem Statement:

The rapid proliferation of digital technologies, data, and algorithms (Level 0 New Reality) presents phenomena that traditional STS methods and concepts (Level 1 Existing Models) may struggle to fully capture, while purely technical or computational analyses often lack critical socio-technical awareness (Level 3 Disciplinary Gaps). This risks either applying inadequate STS frameworks or accepting digital methods uncritically. "Digital STS" emerges to address this gap by demonstrating how STS sensibilities can be productively applied *to* digital phenomena and how digital methods/data can be critically employed *for* STS research (Level 2 Integrated Practice), challenging disciplinary boundaries that obscure the complex co-production happening at this intersection (Level 4 Systemic Need for Integration).

1. Core Model and Index

Vertesi, J., & Ribes, D. (Eds.). (2019). Digital STS. Princeton University Press.

MODEL: Collects essays that map the emerging field of "Digital STS," showcasing how STS theories and methods can be applied to digital phenomena (algorithms, data, platforms, infrastructure) and how digital tools and data can, in turn, reshape STS methodologies and objects of study.

SPATIOTEMPORAL FRAMEWORK

◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The contemporary academic landscape where Science & Technology Studies intersects with the proliferation of digital technologies, data, and methods.

→ [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The reciprocal relationship between STS as an analytical framework and the digital world (as object of study and source of methods).

→ [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Digital artifacts/systems (algorithms, platforms, data) × STS theories/concepts × Digital research methods × STS research practices × Time (emergence of the subfield).

→ [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Scholarly articles applying STS to digital phenomena, studies using digital methods for STS research, debates about methodology, new theoretical concepts emerging from the intersection. (ℝᵏ representing citations, method usage, thematic clusters).

→ [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Development of new digital technologies requiring STS analysis, creation of new digital tools usable by STS scholars, critiques of digital methods from STS perspectives (and vice versa).

→ [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Diverse perspectives within STS engaged with digital technology; computational social science perspectives engaging with STS concepts.

→ [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Mapping the contours and defining the scope of the emerging interdisciplinary field of "Digital STS."

→ [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Focuses specifically on work that bridges STS and the digital (either object or method); distinguishes itself from purely technical digital studies or purely traditional STS.

→ [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: A curated collection of essays showcasing different facets of Digital STS research (e.g., studying algorithms ethnographically, using network analysis for historical STS, critical data studies).

→ [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: "Digital STS" as a dynamic and expanding field where STS sensibility critically informs the study of digital technologies, infrastructures, and data, AND where digital tools and data sources are creatively mobilized to pursue STS research agendas in novel ways.

◻ ENGINE: Field Mapping / Synthesis of Digital STS Approaches.

→ [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Demonstrating how core STS concepts (social shaping, infrastructure, entanglement, situatedness) illuminate digital phenomena, AND how digital methods can provide new traction on STS research questions.

→ [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific digital technologies (e.g., facial recognition), datasets (e.g., social media data), or research methods (e.g., topic modeling) examined through an STS lens or used for STS purposes.

→ [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: Identification of key themes and methodologies characterizing Digital STS; demonstration of the mutual benefits of integrating STS and digital studies/methods.

→ [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on work that explicitly bridges the two domains, highlighting the points of productive friction and synergy.

→ [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: The edited volume itself serves as a representation and definition-by-example of the Digital STS field.

→ [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: How successful Digital STS studies can refine both STS theory and digital methods, opening up new avenues for research at the intersection.

→ [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Broad range of STS theories (ANT, SCOT, feminist STS, critical data studies, infrastructure studies, etc.), computational social science methods, digital humanities techniques.

→ [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Novelty of insights generated, effectiveness of methodological integration, contribution to understanding digital phenomena sociotechnically (qualitative assessment of the field's vitality).

→ [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Establishing Digital STS as a recognized, coherent, and generative interdisciplinary field with its own objects, methods, and theoretical contributions.

METAMODEL

Distinctions & Crossings:

◻ MODEL (Established STS theories / Standard digital methods) | ◻ REALITY (Complex digital phenomena / Social processes amenable to digital tracing) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (Innovative Digital STS research findings, new hybrid methodologies, critical analyses of digital systems informed by STS).

◻ TIME-LIKE (Historical development of STS and digital technologies/methods) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The contemporary landscape of digital platforms/data; the structure of STS theories) → ◻ CAUSAL CONE (How evolving STS theories (time) are being applied to analyze current digital structures (space), and how new digital methods (time) are reshaping STS research practices (space/time)).

◻ CONTINUOUS (The fluid entanglement of social and technical in digital world) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific digital platforms/algorithms studied; distinct STS concepts applied; discrete outputs of computational methods) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Digital STS research making analytical choices to apply specific STS concepts to bounded digital cases, or to use discrete digital methods to analyze complex social phenomena).

Operations: [Observe] digital phenomena through STS lenses OR social phenomena through digital methods. [Encode] findings using hybrid conceptual/methodological frameworks. [Convolve] STS theory (KERNEL) with digital cases (PATCH) OR digital methods (KERNEL) with STS questions (PATCH). [Entangle] STS analysis with digital materiality and methods. [Forget] strict disciplinary separation between STS and digital scholarship/tools.
