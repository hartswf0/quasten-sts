Focused Problem Statement:

The intellectual framework of Modernity (the "Modern Constitution" - Level 1 Model) is built upon a fundamental, enforced separation between Nature (studied by science) and Society/Culture (governed by politics). This act of "Purification" (Level 4 Logic) presents a clean division that obscures the simultaneous and accelerating practice of "Translation" – the creation of vast networks of Nature-Culture hybrids (technologies, environmental problems, socio-technical systems - Level 0 Reality). By denying the existence and proliferation of these hybrids (Level 2 Practice), the Modern framework prevents us from adequately understanding or governing our world, leading to crises born from this contradiction (Level 3 Critique by Latour).

1. Core Model and Index

Latour, B. (1993). We have never been modern. Harvard University Press.

MODEL: Critiques the foundational "modern constitution" that separates Nature and Society (and non-humans from humans) through acts of "purification," while simultaneously creating ever more hybrid networks ("translation") that mix them; argues we must abandon this false dichotomy to understand our world.

Index:

◻ Modernity → (based on a constitution separating) → ◻ Nature ↔ ◻ Society

◻ Purification (separation) → (is contradicted by) → ◻ Translation (hybrid creation)

◻ Hybrids (Nature-Culture mixtures) → (proliferate despite) → ◻ Modern Constitution

◻ "We Have Never Been Modern" → (means) → ◻ The separation was never absolute/real

3. Spatiotemporal Framework

SPATIOTEMPORAL FRAMEWORK

◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The "Modern" era and its accompanying intellectual framework (the "Modern Constitution").

→ [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Practices of knowledge production, political organization, and reality construction characteristic of Modernity.

→ [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: The separated poles of Nature (non-humans, science) & Society/Culture (humans, politics) × Practices of Purification (creating the separation) × Practices of Translation/Mediation (creating hybrids) × Time (Modern epoch).

→ [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Scientific facts, technological artifacts, political institutions, philosophical arguments about nature/culture divide, environmental crises, socio-technical controversies, networks of hybrids. (ℝᵏ representing purity of categories, number/complexity of hybrids).

→ [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Proliferation of obvious hybrids (ozone hole, GMOs, socio-technical systems) that challenge the Nature/Society divide; critiques of Modernity.

→ [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Symmetrical Anthropology / Actor-Network Theory applied historically and philosophically.

→ [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Macro-historical and philosophical critique of the foundational assumptions of Modernity.

→ [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Focuses on the simultaneous processes of separating Nature and Culture (Purification) and mixing them (Translation) that define the Modern condition, arguing the separation was never truly achieved.

→ [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Analysis of key Modern thinkers (Hobbes, Boyle), scientific controversies (pasteurization), technological systems, environmental problems as evidence of hybridity and the failure of Purification.

→ [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: The paradoxical nature of Modernity: Its official "Constitution" rigorously separates Nature and Society, while its actual practices relentlessly create ever more complex Nature-Culture hybrids ("monsters"). The claim is "We Have Never Been Modern" because this separation was always contradicted in practice.

◻ ENGINE: Symmetrical Anthropological Critique of Modernity.

→ [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Analyzing Modern practices symmetrically, showing how Purification (separation) and Translation (hybridization) are two sides of the same coin, and how the latter has proliferated uncontrollably while being officially denied.

→ [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: A specific scientific discovery, technological system, political event, or philosophical argument from the Modern era (e.g., Boyle's air pump experiments, Pasteur's microbes, the ozone hole).

→ [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: Identification of the simultaneous work of Purification and Translation; the inherent contradiction within the Modern Constitution; the proliferation of hybrids.

→ [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the relationship between the official discourse of separation and the practical reality of network-building/hybridization.

→ [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: The model of the Modern Constitution (with its guarantees and paradoxes); critique of modern epistemology and political theory; call for a "nonmodern" or "amodern" perspective recognizing networks of hybrids.

→ [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: How the very success of Modern science and technology (Translation/hybrid creation) increasingly undermines the official ideology of separation (Purification), leading to crises.

→ [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Actor-Network Theory, anthropology (studies of pre-modern cosmologies), history/philosophy of science (Boyle/Hobbes studies), semiotics.

→ [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of separation vs. hybridization, scale/complexity of hybrid networks, severity of crises generated by mismatch between Constitution and practice (qualitative historical/philosophical).

→ [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Recognition of the failure of the Modern project of Purification and the need for a new ("nonmodern") framework capable of dealing explicitly with quasi-objects and quasi-subjects (networks of hybrids).

METAMODEL

Distinctions & Crossings:

◻ MODEL (The Modern Constitution - the official separation of Nature and Society) | ◻ REALITY (The actual proliferation of Nature-Culture hybrids through scientific and technical practice) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (Hybrids like GMOs, global warming, AI – phenomena that straddle the divide and make the failure of the Modern model visible).

◻ TIME-LIKE (The historical unfolding of Modernity, accelerating hybridization) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The conceptual structure of the Modern Constitution separating Nature/Society) → ◻ CAUSAL CONE (How the flawed Constitution (space-like) has guided practices over time, leading to an unsustainable proliferation of unacknowledged hybrids).

◻ CONTINUOUS (The interconnectedness of all entities in networks) | ◻ DISCRETE (The Modern insistence on pure categories of Nature vs. Society) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (The analytical work of Purification that attempts to impose discrete categories onto continuous networks, and the work of Translation that undoes it).

Operations: [Observe] scientific, technical, political practices and discourses of Modernity. [Encode] them in terms of Purification vs. Translation. [Convolve] specific hybrids/controversies (PATCH) with the Modern Constitution framework (KERNEL) to reveal the inherent contradiction. [Entangle] Nature and Society, science and politics, humans and non-humans within networks. [Forget] taking the Modern separation of Nature and Culture at face value.
