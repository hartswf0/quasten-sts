Focused Problem Statement:

Dominant narratives of the Anthropocene (often framed as either techno-optimist salvation or apocalyptic despair - Level 3 Framing) rely on models of human exceptionalism and control (Level 1 Models) that obscure the complex, messy, ongoing reality of multispecies entanglement, co-dependence, and shared vulnerability within damaged ecosystems (Level 0 Reality). By focusing on grand narratives and human agency alone, these frameworks mask the importance of situated, relational practices of "staying with the trouble" – cultivating "response-ability," "making kin," and fostering modest flourishing through "sympoiesis" (co-making) with diverse human and non-human others (Level 2 Practice neglected, Level 4 Logic critiqued).

1. Core Model and Index

Haraway, D. (2016). Staying with the trouble. Duke University Press.

MODEL: Advocates for "staying with the trouble" of living and dying well together in complex, damaged worlds (the Chthulucene), emphasizing multispecies entanglements, response-ability, situated knowledge, and speculative fabulation (SF) as ways to cultivate care and flourishing amidst ongoing ecological and social crises, rejecting easy solutions or apocalyptic narratives.

Index:

◻ Trouble (Complexity, Crisis, Entanglement) → (requires) → ◻ Staying With / Response-ability

◻ Multispecies Flourishing → (depends on) → ◻ Co-becoming / Shared Vulnerability

◻ Chthulucene → (as epoch emphasizing) → ◻ Entanglement / Terrestrial Connections

◻ Speculative Fabulation (SF) → (is a method for) → ◻ Thinking / Making Kin Differently

3. Spatiotemporal Framework

21. Haraway, Donna - Staying With the Trouble (2016)

SPATIOTEMPORAL FRAMEWORK

◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The contemporary epoch of ecological devastation and multispecies entanglement (termed Anthropocene, Capitalocene, Plantationocene, but favoring "Chthulucene" for its emphasis on earthly, chthonic forces).

→ [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Complex, dynamic, and damaged ecological and socio-technical assemblages; sites of multispecies interaction, co-habitation, conflict, and co-evolution.

→ [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Entangled agencies (human, non-human, technological) × Specific places/ecologies × Historical legacies (colonialism, capitalism) × Time (ongoing, generative "thick present").

→ [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Stories, practices of relating (kin-making), scientific studies of sympoiesis, art practices, activist interventions, signs of multispecies flourishing or suffering, emergent patterns in complex systems. (ℝᵏ representing species interactions, narrative tropes, ethical commitments).

→ [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Extinctions, environmental catastrophes, new technologies, ethical shifts, artistic/activist interventions that change relations, acts of making kin differently.

→ [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Feminist multispecies STS, speculative fabulation (SF), process philosophy, environmental humanities, focus on "response-ability" and situated practices.

→ [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Situated, meso-level focus on specific entanglements and practices (pigeon fanciers, Wolbachia-infected insects, activists), resisting both universal abstraction and individualistic focus.

→ [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Rejects human exceptionalism and technological salvation narratives; focuses on ongoing, messy, relational processes of living and dying well together.

→ [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Case studies and stories involving pigeons, corals, dogs, specific ecosystems, art-activist projects (e.g., Fab Lab), weaving string figures (SF) as method.

→ [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: "Staying with the trouble" – the ethico-political imperative to remain situated within, and accountable to, the complex, messy, and ongoing crises of the Chthulucene, cultivating multispecies "response-ability," making kin across boundaries, and fostering modest possibilities for flourishing within damaged worlds.

◻ ENGINE: Thinking/Making Kin/Telling Stories Otherwise (via SF: Speculative Fabulation, String Figures, Science Fiction, So Far).

→ [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Shifting focus from individuals (autopoiesis) to relational becoming (sympoiesis); from grand narratives (progress, apocalypse) to situated, ongoing practices; from critique alone to generative worlding.

→ [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: A specific entanglement, story, practice, or relationship involving multiple actors/species (e.g., training pigeons, CRISPR controversies, activist art).

→ [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The recognition of shared vulnerability and interdependence; the call for response-ability; the potential for unexpected kin-making; the importance of situated storytelling.

→ [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the relational "knots" and generative processes, embracing partiality and complexity over simple solutions or judgments.

→ [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: Concepts like "Chthulucene," "sympoiesis," "making kin," "response-ability," "SF"; stories and analyses that model ways of staying with the trouble.

→ [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Generative feedback loops where practices of relating, storytelling (SF), and thinking differently co-create possibilities for more livable futures within troubled presents.

→ [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Feminist STS (esp. cyborg/companion species thinking), process philosophy (Whitehead), environmental humanities, Indigenous cosmologies (indirectly), science fiction.

→ [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Increased response-ability, flourishing of unexpected relations, generation of livable stories and practices, ethical accountability (qualitative).

→ [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Not a final state, but an ongoing dynamic practice of cultivating care, curiosity, and accountability within complex, ever-changing multispecies entanglements.

◻ CONTINUOUS (Flow of relations, ecological processes, becoming) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific actors, species, stories, practices, ethical decisions identified for analysis/action) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (The analytical and ethical act of focusing on specific knots/stories/practices within the overwhelming continuous entanglement).

[Convolve] specific cases/stories (PATCH) with the Chthulucene framework (KERNEL) to reveal entanglements and ethical demands. [Entangle] ontology, epistemology, ethics, storytelling, practice. [Forget] human exceptionalism, simple solutions, dreams of purity or escape, focusing solely on critique without generation.
