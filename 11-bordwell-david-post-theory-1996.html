Bordwell David Post Theory 1996

1. Problem Statement

  1. Bordwell, David - "Post Theory" (1996)

Focused Problem Statement: The application of "Grand Theory" like psychoanalysis (L1 Interpretive Framework) by scholars (L2) to interpret films like Hitchcock's (L0 Reality) often presents itself (L3 Framing by proponents) as uncovering deep, universal truths. This obscures, as Bordwell argues (L3 Critique), how the framework might filter evidence and lack empirical grounding, reflecting disciplinary norms (L4) that privilege theoretical sophistication over verifiable explanation.

2. Core Model

11. Bordwell, David - "Post Theory" (1996)

  • MODEL: Critiques the dominance of "Grand Theory" (psychoanalysis, structuralism, post-structuralism, etc.) in film studies, advocating for a return to "middle-level research," empirical analysis, historical poetics, and cognitive approaches to understand filmmaking and spectatorship.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Film Studies → ◻ Dominance of "Grand Theory"
    • ◻ "Grand Theory" → ◻ (Critiqued by Bordwell)
    • ◻ Bordwell → ◻ Advocacy for Middle-Level Research
    • ◻ Middle-Level Research → ◻ Empirical Analysis / Historical Poetics / Cognitive Approaches
    • ◻ Middle-Level Research → ◻ Understanding Filmmaking/Spectatorship

3. Four-Level Analysis

11. Bordwell, David - "Post Theory" (1996)

System: A specific psychoanalytic ("Theory"-based) interpretation of a Hitchcock film.

    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Dominant theoretical paradigms ("Theory" - psychoanalysis, structuralism, etc.) and alternative approaches in film analysis.

    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Pragmatist philosophy of science, cognitive science, historical poetics, critique of "Grand Theory."
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Meta-theoretical analysis of the discipline's methodologies and assumptions.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Focuses on the perceived shortcomings of dominant "Theory," advocates for specific alternative ("middle-level") research programs.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Examples of specific "Theory"-driven analyses, contrasting them with historical poetics or cognitive approaches.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Critique of "Theory" & Call for Middle-Level Research – Argues that dominant "Grand Theory" in film studies is often unproductive, unverifiable, and ignores empirical evidence; advocates for more focused, problem-driven "middle-level" research like historical poetics and cognitive analysis.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Disciplinary Critique / Methodological Argumentation.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Evaluating theoretical approaches based on criteria like problem-solving capacity, empirical grounding, testability, explanatory scope.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific theoretical claims or analytical methods from "Theory."

    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Applying the critique to various strands of "Theory," promoting alternative research agendas.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Pragmatist epistemology, cognitive science models of perception/comprehension, historical research methods.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Explanatory power, empirical testability, clarity, problem-solving effectiveness (criteria for evaluating research).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A reorientation of Film Studies towards more "piecemeal," empirically grounded, and cognitively plausible research.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: A framework for evaluating academic research paradigms in Film Studies. Governs the critique of "Theory" and promotion of alternatives. Binds Research Questions/Problems DOMAIN ↔ Methodological Approaches/Explanations CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL ("Theory" frameworks vs. Middle-level research models) | ◻ REALITY (Actual films, spectator responses, historical facts) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (Argues "Theory" often produces artifacts reflecting the theory itself, not reality, while middle-level research aims for artifacts closer to reality).

    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Flow of film experience) | ◻ DISCRETE (Analytical categories, theoretical constructs) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Critiques "Theory" for imposing discrete categories inappropriately; advocates for categories derived from observation).
  • Operations: Observe research practices in film studies. Encode them based on methodological rigor/epistemology. Critiques the Entanglement of certain theories with unverifiable assumptions. Aims to Forget unproductive "Grand Theory."
