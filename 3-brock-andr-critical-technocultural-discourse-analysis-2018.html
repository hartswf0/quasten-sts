Brock Andr Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis 2018

1. Problem Statement

  1. Brock, André - "Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis" (2018)

Focused Problem Statement: The presentation of Google search results (L1 Output) for racially charged queries as objective information retrieval (L4 Ideology of Neutrality) obscures how ranking algorithms (L1 Machine Layer) model relevance in ways that amplify societal racism (L0 Reality) embedded in user search patterns and web content (L2 Human Use). CTDA (L3 Framing) challenges this objectivity by modeling search results as technocultural discourse, revealing how bias is reproduced and naturalized.

2. Core Model

3. Brock, André - "Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis" (2018)

  • MODEL: Proposes a methodology (CTDA) for analyzing digital media and technology that centers the cultural practices and discourses of marginalized groups (particularly Black users), focusing on how technology reflects and shapes their experiences and meanings, often in ways missed by traditional analyses.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Marginalized Groups (esp. Black users) → ◻ Cultural Practices/Discourses
    • ◻ Cultural Practices/Discourses → ◻ Technology Use/Interpretation
    • ◻ Technology → ◻ Shaping User Experience/Meaning
    • ◻ Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis (CTDA) → ◻ Analysis of [Technology Use/Interpretation]

3. Four-Level Analysis

3. Brock, André - "Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis" (2018)

System: Analyzing racist search engine results (e.g., problematic image results for certain queries).

Level 0: <Reality> = <Societal Structures of Racism & Representation>

Level 1: <Machine Layer> = <Search Engine (e.g., Google Search)>
<Search Engine> [simulates] → <Objective Ranking of Information Relevance>
<Search Engine> [outputs] → <Search Engine Results Page (SERP)>
<Search Engine> [filters] → <Indexed Web Content> based on <Ranking Algorithms>

Level 2: <Human Use Layer> = <Users Querying Engine> / <Content Creators optimizing for SEO>
<Users> [prompt] → <Search Engine> with <Queries reflecting societal biases>
<Content Creators> [steer] → <Search Engine> by <Creating biased content that ranks high>

Level 3: <Reflexive Framing Layer> = <Brock (proposing CTDA)>
<Brock> [models] → <Search Results> not as neutral, but as <Technoculturally Produced Discourse reflecting & amplifying bias>
<Brock> [challenges ontology] → <Of Algorithmic Objectivity>
<Brock> [historicizes] → <Search results within lineage of racist representations>

Level 4: <Latent & Systemic Evaluation> = <Ideology of Algorithmic Neutrality / Data Positivism>
<Ideology of Neutrality> [naturalizes] → <Ranked Results as Meritocratic/Objective>
<Ideology of Neutrality> [obscures] → <Human judgments & Societal Biases embedded in Algorithm & Data>
<System Logic> [elevates] → <Correlation/Popularity> over <Social Justice/Representational Equity>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

3. Brock, André - "Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis" (2018)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Technological systems and their societal embedding.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Discourse surrounding and mediated by technology, particularly concerning marginalized groups.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Technological artifacts/platforms & Social contexts × Time.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: User-generated content, interface design, technical documentation, policy documents, media representations of tech. (ℝᵏ representing linguistic features, design elements, power relations).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: New technologies, user resistance/appropriation, policy changes.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: The proposed CTDA methodology itself.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Discourse analysis integrated with technical understanding and critical race/cultural theory.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Prioritizes meaning-making of marginalized users, critiques colorblind/universalist tech perspectives.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Analysis of specific discourses, interfaces, user practices relevant to the group studied.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Technoculturally situated meanings, biases embedded in tech, digital resistance/expression.
  • ◻ ENGINE: CTDA as an analytical engine.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Interpretation of discourse through the lens of critical theories and technological specificity.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Instances of language use, interface design choices, user interactions.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: Underlying assumptions, power dynamics, cultural logics encoded in/expressed through technology.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Synthesizing observations into coherent critiques of technocultural phenomena.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: Arguments about bias, user agency, the co-construction of technology and culture.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Iterative application of the methodology to refine understanding.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Critical Race Theory, STS, Discourse Analysis, Cultural Studies.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Exposure of bias, articulation of marginalized perspectives, explanatory power (qualitative).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A robust, situated explanation of a specific technocultural phenomenon.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: The CTDA framework itself, as a guide for conducting research. Governs the integration of different analytical lenses. Binds Discourse DOMAIN ↔ Technological/Cultural CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (CTDA Framework) | ◻ REALITY (Complex technocultural interactions) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The framework inevitably shapes what is seen).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE | ◻ SPACE-LIKE analysis of discourse evolution and platform structures.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Social forces/cultural context) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific texts/artifacts/features) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD inherent in discourse analysis.
  • Operations: Primarily focuses on defining the Observe, Encode, Convolve, and Entangle operations for studying marginalized technocultures. Aims to minimize Forgetting crucial context.
