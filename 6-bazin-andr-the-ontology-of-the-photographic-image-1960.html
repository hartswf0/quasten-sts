Bazin Andr The Ontology Of The Photographic Image 1960

1. Problem Statement

  1. Bazin, André - “The Ontology of the Photographic Image” (1960)

Focused Problem Statement: Interpreting documentary photographs (e.g., FSA images) solely through their apparent objectivity (L4 Belief) obscures the interplay between the camera's mechanical indexicality (L1 Machine Layer's simulation of L0 Reality) and the crucial filtering choices made by the photographer (L2 Human Use). Bazin's theory (L3 Framing) elevates the indexical trace but potentially under-articulates how L2 interventions shape the "objective window," a tension revealed when analyzing specific photographic practices.

2. Core Model

6. Bazin, André - “The Ontology of the Photographic Image” (1960)

  • MODEL: Argues that photography (and by extension, cinema) possesses a unique ontological status because its mechanical reproduction process creates an image that has an indexical, objective link to the reality it captures, fulfilling a deep human psychological need to preserve time and overcome death.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Reality → ◻ Mechanical Reproduction (Photography/Cinema) → ◻ Photographic Image
    • ◻ Photographic Image → ◻ Indexical Link to Reality
    • ◻ Photographic Image → ◻ Objective Quality
    • ◻ Human Psychology (Preserve Time/Defy Death) → ◻ Desire for Photographic Image

3. Four-Level Analysis

6. Bazin, André - “The Ontology of the Photographic Image” (1960)

System: The creation and viewing of a specific documentary photograph.

Level 0: <Reality> = <The Pro-Filmic Event (the actual scene/person photographed)>

Level 1: <Machine Layer> = <Photographic Camera & Development Process>
<Photographic Apparatus> [simulates] → <Reality via Indexical Trace (light imprint)>
<Photographic Apparatus> [outputs] → <The Photograph>
<Photographic Apparatus> [filters] → <Reality via Lens Optics, Frame, Emulsion Properties>

Level 2: <Human Use Layer> = <Photographer>, <Viewer>
<Photographer> [curates] → <Reality via Framing/Timing>
<Viewer> [interprets] → <The Photograph> as <Objective Window onto Past Reality>

Level 3: <Reflexive Framing Layer> = <Bazin (Film Theorist)>
<Bazin> [models] → <Photography> as <Ontologically Unique due to Mechanical Genesis>
<Bazin> [elevates] → <Indexicality> over <Subjective Interpretation/Style>
<Bazin> [historicizes] → <Photography> as <Fulfillment of Psycho-Historical Desire ('Mummy Complex')>

Level 4: <Latent & Systemic Evaluation> = <Belief in Photographic Objectivity / Psychological Desire for Preservation>
<Belief in Objectivity> [naturalizes] → <The Photograph's Truth Claim>
<Desire for Preservation> [obscures] → <The Constructed & Manipulable Nature of the Photographic Image>
<System Logic> [elevates] → <Mechanical Reproduction> as <Guarantee of Authenticity>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

6. Bazin, André - “The Ontology of the Photographic Image” (1960)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The world of appearances and human attempts to capture it.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Visual representation, particularly mechanical reproduction (photography, cinema).
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Physical reality (objects, scenes) × Historical time (the moment captured).
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Light patterns reflected from reality onto a photosensitive surface. (ℝᵏ representing spatial light intensity/color).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Different photographic processes, manipulation (seen by Bazin as moving away from ontology), aging of the photograph.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Phenomenological and psychological perspective on representation, belief in photographic realism.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the fundamental nature (ontology) of the photographic image itself.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily concerned with the indexical link to reality, less with social construction or specific content (initially).
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: The archetypal photographic act, comparison with painting, mummy preservation (as analogous desires).
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: The Photographic Image as Ontologically Distinct – possessing an objective, indexical bond with the reality it represents due to its mechanical genesis, fulfilling a "mummy complex."
  • ◻ ENGINE: Ontological Argumentation.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Isolating the unique property of photography (mechanical genesis) from other forms of representation.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: The concept/act of taking a photograph.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The indexical link, the trace of reality, the "objective" nature.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the automaticity of the process, downplaying human intervention (framing, developing choices).
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A theory of photographic ontology based on indexicality and psychological need.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Reinforcing the argument by contrasting photography with painting's subjectivity.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Belief in objective reality accessible via mechanical means, psychological theory of preservation desire.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of perceived objectivity, fidelity to the real (ontological, not just visual).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Acceptance of photography's unique status as a trace of the real.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Ontology of Mechanical Reproduction. Governs the understanding of photography's essence. Binds Physical Reality DOMAIN ↔ Photographic Representation CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (The photographic image) | ◻ REALITY (The pro-filmic event) → The crossing (MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT) is minimized; Bazin argues the model (photo) preserves reality indexically.
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Preserving a moment) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The 2D image). The crossing (CAUSAL CONE) is the light traveling from the real event to the film.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Reality's flow) | ◻ DISCRETE (The frozen photographic moment/frame) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (The act of capturing a discrete slice of time-space).
  • Operations: Observe the photographic process. Encode the image as an objective trace. Entangle the image directly with past reality. Tends to Forget the constructedness/manipulability inherent even in analog processes.
