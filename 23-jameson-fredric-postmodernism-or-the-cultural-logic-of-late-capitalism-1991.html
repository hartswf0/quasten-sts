Jameson Fredric Postmodernism Or The Cultural Logic Of Late Capitalism 1991

1. Problem Statement

  1. Jameson, Fredric - Postmodernism, or, The Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism (1991)

Focused Problem Statement: Experiencing the spatial disorientation of the Bonaventure Hotel (L1 Architecture output) merely as quirky design obscures its significance as modeled by Jameson (L3 Framing). He interprets this difficulty in cognitive mapping (L2 User experience) as symptomatic of postmodern hyperspace, a cultural dominant reflecting the fragmentation and abstraction of late capitalism (L4 Base Logic) transforming our core experience of space (L0 Reality).

2. Core Model

23. Jameson, Fredric - Postmodernism, or, The Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism (1991)

  • MODEL: Argues that postmodernism is not just an artistic style but the dominant cultural logic corresponding to the "late capitalist" (multinational, globalized, consumerist) stage of economic development. Characterizes postmodern culture by features like pastiche, fragmentation, depthlessness, waning of affect, and a crisis in historicity.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Late Capitalism (Economic Stage) → ◻ Postmodernism (Cultural Logic)
    • ◻ Postmodernism → ◻ Cultural Dominant
    • ◻ Postmodern Culture → ◻ Characterized by (Pastiche, Fragmentation, Depthlessness, Waning Affect, Crisis in Historicity)

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

23. Jameson, Fredric - Postmodernism, or, The Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism (1991)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Global society under the conditions of late (multinational, consumer) capitalism, roughly from the mid-20th century onwards.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Cultural production (art, architecture, literature, film, theory) as expressive of and embedded within the socio-economic system of late capitalism.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Cultural artifacts, aesthetic styles, theoretical concepts, economic structures (globalization, consumerism), technological changes (media saturation) × Historical period of late capitalism.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Pastiche, fragmentation, depthlessness, waning of affect, nostalgia, schizophrenic temporalities in cultural works; spatial disorientation (e.g., Bonaventure Hotel). (ℝᵏ representing stylistic features, thematic content, affective tone).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Emergence of new technologies, shifts in global power dynamics, attempts at critical resistance (often seen as co-opted).
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Neo-Marxist theory (Althusser, Mandel), psychoanalysis (Lacan), architectural theory, analysis of cultural superstructure in relation to economic base.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Totalizing, systemic analysis linking diverse cultural phenomena to an underlying socio-economic logic ("cultural dominant").
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Focuses on identifying postmodernism as the cultural expression of a specific stage of capitalism, interpreting cultural features symptomatically.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Analysis of key postmodern artworks, architecture, films, literature; theoretical concepts; spatial experiences.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Postmodernism as the Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism – A cultural condition (dominant, not exclusive style) characterized by specific aesthetic features (pastiche, depthlessness, etc.) that reflects and reinforces the logic of the late capitalist socioeconomic system (globalization, commodification of everything, fragmentation of experience, loss of historical connection).
  • ◻ ENGINE: Symptomatic Reading / Homology Mapping / Totalizing Synthesis.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Interpreting cultural features as symptoms or expressions ("homologies") of the underlying logic of late capitalism.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific cultural works, styles, or experiences.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The core features of postmodernism (pastiche, depthlessness, etc.); the underlying logic of late capitalism as the determining force.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting common features across diverse cultural fields and linking them back to the economic base.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A grand narrative linking culture, technology, economics, and subjective experience in the postmodern era. Concept of "cognitive mapping" as a needed response.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Applying the postmodern diagnosis across various cultural domains and geographical contexts.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Marxist theory of base/superstructure/ideology, Lacanian psychoanalysis (schizophrenia, waning affect), theories of historical periodization.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Presence of postmodern features, degree of fragmentation/depthlessness, loss of historicity, difficulty of cognitive mapping (qualitative/symptomatic).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A comprehensive "cognitive map" of the totality of late capitalism and its cultural logic, enabling critique and potential resistance.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Marxist Theory of Cultural Periodization. Governs the linking of economic stages to cultural dominants. Binds Socio-Economic Base (Late Capitalism) DOMAIN ↔ Cultural Superstructure (Postmodernism) CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (Jameson's theory of Postmodernism) | ◻ REALITY (Complex and contradictory cultural/social field) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The theory itself imposes a totalizing logic that might oversimplify or homogenize diverse phenomena).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Loss of historical sense, schizophrenic present) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (Hyperspace, globalization, fragmentation of space). Postmodernism is characterized by a crisis in both temporal and spatial experience, a key crossing point.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Flow of capital, media saturation) | ◻ DISCRETE (Fragmented cultural products, pastiche elements, subjective moments) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Postmodern culture breaks down continuous narratives/experiences into discrete, depthless fragments).
  • Operations: Observe cultural trends and artifacts. Encode them as symptoms of postmodernism/late capitalism. Entangle aesthetic features (FEATURE) with socio-economic structures (FEATURE). Critiques postmodernism's tendency to Forget history and depth.
