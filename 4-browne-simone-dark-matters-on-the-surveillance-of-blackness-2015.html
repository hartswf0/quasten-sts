Browne Simone Dark Matters On The Surveillance Of Blackness 2015

1. Problem Statement

  1. Browne, Simone - Dark Matters: On the Surveillance of Blackness (2015)

Focused Problem Statement: Framing facial recognition systems used in policing (L1 Machine Layer) primarily as neutral security tools (L4 Logic of Control) obscures how their modeling of Black identity (L0 Reality) through biased data and algorithms perpetuates historical anti-Black surveillance practices. Browne's analysis (L3 Framing) historicizes this technology within a genealogy of control ("Dark Matters"), revealing how its differential application (L2 Human Use) reflects and reinforces latent systemic racism (L4).

2. Core Model

4. Browne, Simone - Dark Matters: On the Surveillance of Blackness (2015)

  • MODEL: Argues that contemporary surveillance technologies and practices have historical roots in the surveillance methods used during slavery and continue to disproportionately target and discipline Black life, making Blackness itself a key object and subject of surveillance.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Historical Surveillance (Slavery) → ◻ Contemporary Surveillance Technologies/Practices
    • ◻ Contemporary Surveillance Technologies/Practices → ◻ Targeting/Disciplining Black Life
    • ◻ Blackness → ◻ Object/Subject of Surveillance (Dark Matters)

3. Four-Level Analysis

4. Browne, Simone - Dark Matters: On the Surveillance of Blackness (2015)

System: Biometric identification systems (e.g., facial recognition used in policing).

Level 0: <Reality> = <Black Embodiment & Social Life>

<Facial Recognition System> [simulates] → <Unique & Stable Identity based on Facial Features>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

4. Browne, Simone - Dark Matters: On the Surveillance of Blackness (2015)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Historical and contemporary Western societies, focusing on regimes of control.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Practices and technologies of surveillance, particularly as applied to Black populations.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Social spaces, bodies, digital networks × Historical Time (Slavery to Present).
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Visual data (lantern laws, branding, CCTV, biometrics), tracking data (slave passes, databases), sorting mechanisms. (ℝᵏ representing identity markers, location, risk scores).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: New surveillance tech, acts of resistance ("sousveillance"), legal changes.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Black feminist theory, surveillance studies, critical race theory, performance studies.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Historical genealogy, analysis of specific technologies and practices, focus on the body and visibility/invisibility.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Centers Blackness as the object/subject of surveillance, connects historical to contemporary.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Historical archives (slave laws, branding irons), contemporary tech (biometrics, CCTV), artistic/activist responses.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: "Dark Matters" – the ongoing, racialized surveillance of Blackness; the ways Blackness is rendered visible/invisible for control; acts of refusal and fugitive visibility.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Genealogical and critical analysis.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Tracing historical continuities in surveillance logic, deconstructing the neutrality claims of technology.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific surveillance events, technologies, historical documents, artworks.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The enduring logic of racialized surveillance, the management of visibility/opacity.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Identifying the core principles linking disparate surveillance practices across time.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: Theory of "Dark Matters," demonstrating the historical depth and racial coding of surveillance.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Applying the analytical lens across different historical periods and technological forms.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Foucault's theories of power/knowledge/discipline, Black feminist thought, theories of visuality.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of control, racial disparity, historical continuity, forms of resistance (qualitative).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Recognition of surveillance's deep entanglement with anti-Blackness.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: A critical framework for understanding surveillance through the lens of race and history. Governs the linking of past and present practices. Binds Historical Control DOMAIN ↔ Contemporary Surveillance CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:

  • Operations: Observe practices/technologies of surveillance. Encode them within a framework of racialized control. Entangle historical forms (branding) with contemporary forms (biometrics). Critique the Forgetting of history in tech discourse.
