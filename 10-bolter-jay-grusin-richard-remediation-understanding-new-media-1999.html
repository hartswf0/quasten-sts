Bolter Jay Grusin Richard Remediation Understanding New Media 1999

1. Problem Statement

  1. Bolter, Jay & Grusin, Richard - Remediation: Understanding New Media. (1999)

Focused Problem Statement: Describing early news websites (L1 Machine Layer) simply as "digital newspapers" obscures the complex process of remediation identified by Bolter & Grusin (L3 Framing). The website actively simulates and filters print conventions (L0 Reality), driven by contradictory cultural desires for both transparent immediacy and fascination with the new medium's interface (L4 Logics), shaping user experience (L2) in ways missed by simple equivalence.

2. Core Model

10. Bolter, Jay & Grusin, Richard - Remediation: Understanding New Media. (1999)

  • MODEL: Proposes that new media forms achieve cultural significance by "remediating" older media forms – that is, by borrowing, repurposing, critiquing, and refashioning their conventions – operating under a dual logic of immediacy (trying to erase the medium) and hypermediacy (drawing attention to the medium).
  • Index:
    • ◻ New Media → ◻ Remediation → ◻ Older Media
    • ◻ Remediation → ◻ Borrowing/Repurposing/Critiquing Conventions
    • ◻ New Media → ◻ Logic of Immediacy (Transparency)
    • ◻ New Media → ◻ Logic of Hypermediacy (Opacity/Self-Awareness)

3. Four-Level Analysis

10. Bolter, Jay & Grusin, Richard - Remediation: Understanding New Media. (1999)

System: A website presenting news articles (e.g., NYTimes.com in the late 90s).

Level 0: <Reality> = <The Conventions and Authority of Print Newspapers> & <Desire for Transparent Access to Information>

Level 1: <Machine Layer> = <News Website Interface/Structure>
<Website> [simulates] → <Print Newspaper Layout (columns, headlines) & Immediacy (direct access)> via <Remediation>
<Website> [outputs] → <Hyperlinked Articles, Multimedia Elements, Navigational Structure> (also showing Hypermediacy)
<Website> [filters] → <Print Experience> (losing tactile feel, fixed layout) while adding <New affordances (links, search)>

Level 2: <Human Use Layer> = <User Browsing the Website>, <Website Designers>
<User> [interprets] → <Website> based on <Familiarity with Print Conventions & Digital Navigation>
<Designers> [steer] → <User Experience> through <Interface choices balancing Immediacy & Hypermediacy>

Level 3: <Reflexive Framing Layer> = <Bolter & Grusin (Media Theorists)>
<Bolter & Grusin> [model] → <Website Design> as <Act of Remediation driven by Dual Logics (Immediacy/Hypermediacy)>
<Bolter & Grusin> [historicize] → <New Media Forms> as <Always Referring To/Refashioning Older Media>

Level 4: <Latent & Systemic Evaluation> = <Cultural Logics of Representation / Historical Interplay of Media>
<Logic of Immediacy> [naturalizes] → <Desire for Transparent, Unmediated Access>
<Logic of Hypermediacy> [naturalizes] → <Fascination with the Medium Itself>
<Framework Logic> [obscures] → <Idea of Media Existing in Isolation>, highlighting <Constant Borrowing & Refashioning>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

10. Bolter, Jay & Grusin, Richard - Remediation: Understanding New Media. (1999)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: History of media forms and their interactions, especially the emergence of digital ("new") media.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The interplay and evolution of different media technologies and forms.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Media artifacts (paintings, photos, films, websites, VR), user interfaces × Historical context of media evolution.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Formal properties of media, interface designs, references to older media, user experiences of transparency/opacity. (ℝᵏ representing interface elements, stylistic borrowings, degree of perceived mediation).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Invention of new media, changing aesthetic tastes, user adaptation/resistance to media forms.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Media history, semiotics, analysis of formal properties and cultural logics.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the formal strategies by which media represent reality and other media.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily examines the relationship between media forms, analyzed through the logics of immediacy and hypermediacy.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Examples of websites remediating print, VR remediating film, painting remediating photography (and vice versa), interface designs.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Remediation – The process by which new media refashion, incorporate, critique, and absorb prior media forms, driven by the dual (and often contradictory) cultural logics of immediacy (desire for transparent reality) and hypermediacy (fascination with the medium itself).
  • ◻ ENGINE: Formal Media Analysis / Logic Identification.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Identifying instances and strategies of remediation, classifying them according to the logics of immediacy and hypermediacy.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific media artifacts or interfaces.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The underlying logics of immediacy and hypermediacy; the pattern of remediation as a driving force in media history.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the formal relationship between media, bracketing specific content or social context in the initial analysis.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A theory of media evolution based on the concept of remediation and its dual logics.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Applying the remediation framework across different media and historical periods.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Theories of media specificity, representation, cultural history.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of Immediacy vs. Hypermediacy, type of remediation (e.g., absorption, critique), historical prevalence (qualitative/descriptive).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Understanding any given medium as a point in a continuous process of remediation.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Theory of Remediation. Governs the analysis of inter-medial relationships. Binds Older Media Forms/Logics DOMAIN ↔ Newer Media Forms/Interfaces CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (The remediating medium) | ◻ REALITY (The remediated medium OR the reality it tries to represent) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The way the new medium frames the old, or frames reality, is an artifact of its specific remediation strategy).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Historical sequence of media) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The co-existence and interaction of media forms in culture). The CAUSAL CONE shows influence flowing from older to newer media (and sometimes back).
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (The ongoing cultural desire for immediacy/hypermediacy) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific media artifacts embodying these logics) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Analyzing discrete artifacts to understand the continuous underlying cultural drives).
  • Operations: Observe media forms and interfaces. Encode them based on immediacy/hypermediacy markers and references to other media. Entangle the features of one medium (FEATURE) with the form of another. Helps Forget the idea of media existing in isolation.
