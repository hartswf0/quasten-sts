Dick Higgins Intermedia 2001 Concept 1965

1. Problem Statement

  1. Dick Higgins - "Intermedia" (2001, concept ~1965)

Focused Problem Statement: Classifying artworks strictly within traditional categories like "Poetry" or "Visual Art" (L0 Reality / L4 Taxonomy) obscures the significance of works like Concrete Poetry (L1 Artifact) that deliberately fuse elements from multiple domains. Higgins' concept of "Intermedia" (L3 Framing) models these hybrid forms, revealing how the avant-garde impulse challenges discrete classifications and requires new interpretive strategies (L2) from the audience.

2. Core Model

15. Dick Higgins - "Intermedia" (2001)

  • MODEL: Introduces the concept of "Intermedia" to describe artworks that exist between traditional media categories (e.g., blending poetry and visual art, music and performance), suggesting this blurring of boundaries is a characteristic mode of avant-garde and contemporary artistic practice.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Traditional Art Media (Distinct Categories)
    • ◻ Artistic Practice → ◻ Blurring Boundaries Between Media → ◻ Intermedia
    • ◻ Intermedia → ◻ Characteristic of Avant-Garde/Contemporary Art

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

15. Dick Higgins - "Intermedia" (2001, concept ~1965)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The world of artistic creation, particularly the avant-garde movements of the mid-20th century onwards.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Boundaries and interactions between established art forms (music, painting, poetry, dance, theatre, etc.).
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Art media conventions, materials, techniques × Historical context of avant-garde experimentation.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Artworks exhibiting characteristics of multiple media, theoretical statements about medium boundaries, manifestos. (ℝᵏ representing mixture coefficients of different media characteristics).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Emergence of new technologies enabling new combinations, institutional resistance/acceptance of hybrid forms, theoretical debates about medium specificity.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Perspective of an active practitioner (Fluxus artist), interest in conceptual art, breaking down traditional categories.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on the "gaps" and overlaps between conventional media.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily concerned with avant-garde and experimental practices that challenge genre purity.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Examples like concrete poetry (poetry/visual art), happenings (theatre/visual art/life), sound poetry (music/poetry).
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Intermedia – Artworks or practices that intentionally inhabit the conceptual and practical spaces between traditional, recognized art media, drawing on and fusing elements from multiple forms, often resulting in new hybrid genres.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Categorical Innovation / Boundary Blurring Identification.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Identifying artworks that defy easy categorization within existing media boundaries and labeling this "in-between" space.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific avant-garde artworks or practices.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The concept of "Intermedia" as a recurring mode of artistic innovation; the blurring of medium specificity.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Focusing on the formal mixing of media characteristics, potentially less on the specific content or political import.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A map of the arts showing the "intermedial" spaces between established forms.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Identifying more and more examples and potential zones of intermedia.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: A conceptual model of the arts as distinct fields with potentially fertile border areas.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of hybridity, novelty of combination, resistance to single-medium classification (qualitative).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: Recognition and acceptance of Intermedia as a valid and significant artistic strategy.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Conceptual Map of Artistic Media and their Interfaces. Governs the identification of hybrid forms. Binds Established Media Conventions DOMAIN ↔ Intermedial Practices/Artworks CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (Conceptual map of media/intermedia) | ◻ REALITY (Fluidity of artistic practice) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The term "Intermedia" itself is an artifact, a label trying to capture fluid practices).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Emergence of new intermedial forms) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (The conceptual map of media boundaries). The CAUSAL CONE shows artists crossing space-like boundaries over time.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Spectrum of artistic possibilities) | ◻ DISCRETE (Named media categories, the concept of "Intermedia") → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Naming "Intermedia" creates a discrete category within the continuum).
  • Operations: Observe avant-garde art. Encode works based on their relationship to established media boundaries. Entangle features from different media (FEATURE x FEATURE) to define Intermedia. Aims to Forget rigid genre distinctions.
