Brock A Distributed Blackness African American Cybercultures 2020

1. Problem Statement

  1. Brock, A. - Distributed Blackness: African American Cybercultures (2020)

Focused Problem Statement: Treating platforms like Twitter (L1 Machine Layer) as neutral public spheres obscures the specific ways Black users (L2 Users) perform "Distributed Blackness"—employing distinct cultural logics (L0 Reality) to navigate and reinterpret platform architectures shaped by White technonormativity and platform capitalism (L4 Logic). Brock's CTDA (L3 Framing) models these technocultural practices within specific discourse events (e.g., #SolidarityIsForWhiteWomen), revealing user agency and resistance often missed by universalizing platform analyses.

2. Core Model

2. Brock, A. - Distributed Blackness: African American Cybercultures (2020)

  • MODEL: Analyzes how Black identity is constructed and performed online through specific cultural and technological practices within digital platforms, arguing for a concept of "Distributed Blackness" shaped by both community expression and platform affordances/biases.
  • Index:
    • ◻ Black Identity → ◻ Online Performance/Construction
    • ◻ Online Performance/Construction → ◻ African American Cybercultures (Distributed Blackness)
    • ◻ Digital Platforms (Affordances/Biases) → ◻ African American Cybercultures
    • ◻ Community Expression/Cultural Practices → ◻ African American Cybercultures

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

2. Brock, A. - Distributed Blackness: African American Cybercultures (2020)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: Contemporary digital networked environments.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: African American discourse and cultural practice online.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Digital platforms (Twitter, blogs etc.) × Real-time interaction & historical context.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Tweets, memes, linguistic styles (AAVE online), hashtags, user profiles, platform interface elements. (ℝᵏ representing linguistic features, network connections, thematic content).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Platform algorithm changes, user innovation (e.g., Black Twitter), socio-political events, moderation policies.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Critical Technocultural Discourse Analysis (CTDA).
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Micro-level discourse, platform affordances, community norms.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Focus on Black users' experiences, meaning-making, and interaction with and through technology.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Specific hashtags, user networks, platform feature analysis, discourse patterns.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: "Distributed Blackness" – identity performed and negotiated across networks, shaped by both cultural logics and technological structures (including bias).
  • ◻ ENGINE: CTDA Methodology.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Linking specific discursive acts to technological features and broader cultural/racial contexts.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific online interactions, user interfaces, platform policies.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: Underlying principles of Black cultural expression online, technocultural logics, platform biases.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Identifying recurring patterns of discourse and interaction.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: Concepts like "Distributed Blackness," analyses of specific online phenomena (e.g., hashtag activism).
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Iterative analysis refining the understanding of the interplay between discourse, technology, and culture.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Theories of race, language, technology, discourse analysis, cultural studies.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Resonance, resistance, community formation, influence of platform design (qualitative/quantitative).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: A stable understanding of how Blackness is articulated and mediated technologically in specific contexts.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Framework for analyzing marginalized technocultures. Governs analysis across different platforms/discourses. Binds Technological DOMAIN ↔ Cultural/Discursive CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (Academic analysis/CTDA) | ◻ REALITY (Lived experience/User interpretations) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (The challenge of representing authentic user meaning accurately).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Evolution of discourse/platforms) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (Network structures/Platform architecture) → The CAUSAL CONE of influence between user action and platform response.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Cultural flow/Identity fluidity) | ◻ DISCRETE (Tweets, posts, platform features) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (How discrete digital artifacts represent continuous cultural life).
  • Operations: Observe online activity/platform design. Encode discourse using linguistic/cultural frameworks. Convolve user actions with platform KERNEL (affordances/algorithms). Entangle technology and cultural expression.
