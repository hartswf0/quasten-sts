Bazin Andr The Myth Of Total Cinema 1967

1. Problem Statement

  1. Bazin, André - “The Myth of Total Cinema” (1967)

Focused Problem Statement: Linear histories often present cinema's technological development (e.g., sound, color) (L1 Machine Layer) simply as technical problem-solving towards better realism (L0 Perceptual Reality). This obscures the driving force of what Bazin (L3 Framing) models as the "Myth of Total Cinema"—an underlying psycho-cultural desire (L4 Latent Idealism) shaping inventors' and filmmakers' goals (L2 Human Use), thus framing technical steps as teleological fulfillment rather than contingent history.

2. Core Model

7. Bazin, André - “The Myth of Total Cinema” (1967)

  • MODEL: Posits that the development of cinema was driven less by specific technological inventions and more by a persistent human desire (a "myth") for a complete and perfect audiovisual reproduction of reality – "total cinema."
  • Index:
    • ◻ Human Desire/Psychology → ◻ Myth of Total Cinema (Perfect Reality Reproduction)
    • ◻ Myth of Total Cinema → ◻ Driving Force for Cinema's Development
    • ◻ Technological Inventions → ◻ (Subordinate to) Myth of Total Cinema

3. Four-Level Analysis

7. Bazin, André - “The Myth of Total Cinema” (1967)

System: The historical development of cinematic realism (e.g., addition of sound, color).

Level 0: <Reality> = <Human Perceptual Experience of the World>

Level 1: <Machine Layer> = <Cinema Technology (evolving apparatus)>
<Cinema Technology> [simulates] → <Aspects of Perceptual Reality (sound, color, depth)> incrementally
<Cinema Technology> [outputs] → <More 'Realistic' Film Experiences>
<Cinema Technology> [filters] → <Which Aspects of Reality are Prioritized for Replication>

Level 2: <Human Use Layer> = <Inventors>, <Filmmakers>, <Audiences>
<Inventors/Filmmakers> [steer] → <Technological Development> towards <Greater Illusionism>
<Audiences> [interpret] → <Technological Advances> as <Progress Towards Realism>

Level 3: <Reflexive Framing Layer> = <Bazin (Theorist/Historian)>
<Bazin> [models] → <Technological Development> as <Driven by Pre-existing Ideal ('Myth of Total Cinema')>
<Bazin> [historicizes] → <Inventions> as <Manifestations of this Underlying Myth>

Level 4: <Latent & Systemic Evaluation> = <Teleological Idealism / Psychological Essentialism>
<Teleological Idealism> [naturalizes] → <Progress Towards Realism as Inevitable Goal>
<Psychological Essentialism> [obscures] → <Contingent Social, Economic, & Accidental Factors in Invention>
<Framework> [elevates] → <Underlying Myth> over <Material History>

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

7. Bazin, André - “The Myth of Total Cinema” (1967)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: History of technological development, specifically related to audiovisual representation.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: The evolution of cinematic technology and aesthetics.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Technological inventions (camera, sound, color) × Historical Time (19th C onwards).
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Films themselves, technical specifications, writings of inventors/theorists. (ℝᵏ representing fidelity features like resolution, sound quality, color accuracy).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Specific inventions, aesthetic movements (e.g., montage vs. realism), economic factors influencing development.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Idealist, teleological view of technological history; focus on underlying psychological drives.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Broad historical sweep, focus on guiding ideals rather than contingent factors.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Privileges the pursuit of realism as the driving force, downplays other motivations (economic, experimental).
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Key inventions (photography, phonograph, projector), statements about the aims of cinema.
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: The "Myth of Total Cinema" – an inherent human desire for a complete audiovisual recreation of reality that pre-exists and guides the actual technical inventions, giving cinema its teleological drive towards greater realism.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Teleological Historical Argument.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Interpreting technological developments as steps towards a pre-ordained goal (Total Cinema).
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific inventions or aesthetic choices.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The underlying "myth" or psychological drive for integral realism.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting from contingent historical details to fit the overarching narrative of the myth.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A historical narrative where technology fulfills a pre-existing psychological need/ideal.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Seeing each new realistic technology (sound, color, widescreen) as further proof of the myth's power.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Idealist philosophy, psychological essentialism (belief in a fixed human desire).
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Closeness to achieving "Total Cinema" (qualitative assessment of realism).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: The (never fully attainable) ideal of Total Cinema itself.
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Idealist History of Cinema. Governs the interpretation of technological progress. Binds Human Psyche DOMAIN ↔ Cinematic Technology/Aesthetics CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:
    • ◻ MODEL (The Myth of Total Cinema) | ◻ REALITY (Contingent history of invention) → ◻ MEASUREMENT ARTIFACT (Historical events are interpreted through the lens of the myth, making the myth seem like the cause).
    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Historical progression towards the ideal) is the central axis.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (The enduring psychological myth) | ◻ DISCRETE (Specific inventions) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Inventions are seen as discrete steps realizing the continuous underlying desire).
  • Operations: Observe cinematic history. Encode inventions as expressions of the myth. Entangle technology with psychological drives. Tends to Forget economic/social/accidental factors in invention.
