Murray Janet Hamlet On The Holodeck 1997 Revised 2017

1. Problem Statement

  1. Murray, Janet - Hamlet on the Holodeck (1997, revised 2017)

Focused Problem Statement: Focusing solely on pre-scripted branching narratives when analyzing game storytelling obscures the emergent narrative potential of highly procedural games like Dwarf Fortress (L1 Machine Layer). While Murray (L3 Framing) emphasizes agency and immersion, analyzing how players (L2) derive narrative from complex simulations modeling systems (L0 Reality) challenges and potentially extends her aesthetic categories, reflecting latent beliefs in procedural expressivity (L4).

2. Core Model

31. Murray, Janet - Hamlet on the Holodeck (1997, revised 2017)

  • MODEL: Explores the potential of digital media (computers, interactive environments) as a new narrative medium. Argues that its defining aesthetic properties are immersion, agency, and transformation, allowing for new forms of storytelling that resemble the immersive, participatory experience fantasized in Star Trek's "holodeck."
  • Index:
    • ◻ Digital Media → ◻ Potential as New Narrative Medium
    • ◻ Digital Narrative Aesthetics → ◻ Properties (Immersion, Agency, Transformation)
    • ◻ Digital Narrative → ◻ Enabling New Storytelling Forms (Participatory, Immersive)
    • ◻ Holodeck (Metaphor) → ◻ Idealized Digital Narrative Experience

4. Spatiotemporal Framework

31. Murray, Janet - Hamlet on the Holodeck (1997, revised 2017)

  • ◻ SPATIOTEMPORAL UNIVERSE: The emerging landscape of digital interactive technologies and their narrative potential.
    • → [Contains] → ◻ FIELD: Digital environments (computers, games, VR) as potential media for storytelling and artistic expression.
      • → [Has] → ◻ DOMAIN: Computer hardware/software, interactive interfaces, algorithms, databases, virtual environments × User interaction patterns and aesthetic conventions.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ SIGNAL: Interactive narratives, game mechanics allowing agency, immersive virtual worlds, procedural content generation, user choices and their consequences. (ℝᵏ representing degrees of freedom, immersion level, narrative branching complexity).
      • → [Admits] → ◻ PERTURBATION: Advances in computing power, VR/AR technology, AI for character behavior, evolving design conventions for interactivity.
    • → [Observed Through] → ◻ WINDOW: Literary theory (narrative, aesthetics), computer science (HCI, AI), media studies, optimistic view of technology's artistic potential.
      • → [Defines] → ◻ OBSERVATION SCALE: Focus on identifying the unique aesthetic properties and narrative capabilities inherent in the digital medium.
      • → [Delimits] → ◻ CONTEXT BOUNDARY: Primarily concerned with the potential for rich, complex interactive narratives, using traditional narrative arts as reference points but seeking digital specificity.
      • → [Generates] → ◻ SAMPLING LATTICE: Analysis of early interactive fiction, video games (adventure, RPGs), hypertexts, virtual reality experiments, envisioning future possibilities (the "Holodeck").
    • → [Manifests As] → ◻ PHENOMENON: Digital Medium's Narrative Aesthetics (Immersion, Agency, Transformation) – Digital environments offer unique aesthetic pleasures crucial for storytelling: Immersion (feeling present in a virtual world), Agency (ability to make meaningful choices affecting the narrative), and Transformation (shape-shifting nature of digital objects and identities). These properties enable new forms of narrative experience.
  • ◻ ENGINE: Aesthetic Property Identification / Comparative Media Poetics.
    • → [Applies] → ◻ TRANSFORM: Analyzing digital artifacts and interactions to identify their core aesthetic capabilities, particularly in relation to traditional narrative forms.
      • → [Operates On] → ◻ PATCH: Specific interactive systems, game mechanics, virtual environment designs, user experiences.
      • → [Produces] → ◻ INVARIANT: The key aesthetic properties: Immersion, Agency, Transformation; understanding of procedurality as an expressive tool.
      • → [Requires] → ◻ CONTRACTION: Abstracting from specific implementations to the underlying aesthetic principles enabled by digital technology.
    • → [Constructs] → ◻ REPRESENTATION: A theoretical framework (a poetics) for understanding and designing interactive digital narratives.
    • → [Optimizes] → ◻ RECURSION: Applying the concepts of immersion, agency, and transformation to analyze existing works and envision future possibilities.
      • → [Embeds] → ◻ MODEL: Theories of narrative (Aristotle onwards), aesthetics, HCI principles, concepts of play and simulation.
      • → [Projects] → ◻ METRIC: Degree of immersion, meaningfulness of agency, richness of transformation, narrative coherence/complexity (qualitative design goals).
      • → [Seeks] → ◻ FIXED POINT: The realization of sophisticated, aesthetically rich interactive narratives that fully exploit the digital medium's potential (the "Hamlet on the Holodeck").
  • ◻ METAMODEL: Poetics of Interactive Digital Narrative. Governs the analysis and design of digital storytelling. Binds Computational Properties (Interactivity, Procedurality) DOMAIN ↔ Narrative Aesthetics (Immersion, Agency, Transformation) CODMAIN.
  • Distinctions & Crossings:

    • ◻ TIME-LIKE (Narrative unfolding based on user agency) | ◻ SPACE-LIKE (Immersive virtual environment, navigable space). Focuses on the interplay: user actions in space drive narrative time.
    • ◻ CONTINUOUS (Ideal immersive experience, story flow) | ◻ DISCRETE (User choices, code execution, procedural rules) → ◻ SAMPLING THRESHOLD (Aims to use discrete computations to create a feeling of continuous immersion and agency).
  • Operations: Observe interactive digital media. Encode their features based on immersion, agency, transformation. Convolve user actions (PATCH) with procedural rules (KERNEL) to create narrative events (FEATURE). Entangle computation (FEATURE) with aesthetic experience (FEATURE).
